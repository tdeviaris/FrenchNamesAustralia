Cap Boileau - Cape Boileau

The inclusion of Nicolas Boileau in the nomenclature is not fortuitous. It reflects the role played by the Institut de France in the valorisation of men of science, such as Charles-Augustin de Coulomb, Lazare Carnot, Pierre André Latreille (from 1814), and Bernard Germain de Lacépède, whose names appear in the vicinity of Cape Boileau, as well as of men of letters.

Between 1801 and 1805, the Institut organised an annual concours d’éloquence, a highly regarded poetry competition intended to affirm the status of writers. The subject “Éloge de Boileau” was proposed on four occasions.

Boileau himself considered that satire could help correct the errors of his time:

“Satire, rich in lessons and in novelty, alone knows how to combine the pleasant with the useful, and, through verses refined by the light of reason, to free minds from the errors of their age” (Satire IX, To his mind).
