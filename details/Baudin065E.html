Péron, who had himself been involved in a doomed romance with a young woman, ‘Sophie’, who had already been promised to another man, was probably very receptive of this novel and its irreconcilable and impassioned love story.

Further information: Baudin named several geographical features south of this cape, but they were likely omitted by Péron and Freycinet due to uncertainty about the accuracy of the bearings taken in April 1802. Baudin’s names, Pointe des bouquets, Anse de la verdure and Pointe aux arbres, suggest that the coast was covered with a rich vegetation. Péron explains that ‘It is at this last height that le Géographe* had been forced, the previous year, to finish his surveys, and that le Casuarina* had to start his’ (‘C'est à cette dernière hauteur que le Géographe avoit été contraint, l'année précédente, de terminer ses relèvemens, et que le Casuarina devoit commencer les siens. ’, p. 87)
