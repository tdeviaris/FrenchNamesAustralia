The marriage of Princess Auguste Amélie of Bavaria to Prince Eugène had a highly significant diplomatic dimension: it sealed the union between the Kingdom of Bavaria and the Kingdom of Italy, of which Prince Eugène was the viceroy. Through this alliance, the aim was to prevent the Austrians from accessing the passes located on either side of the Alps. The religious ceremony was officiated by Baron von Dalberg, who would soon become the Prince-Primate of the Confederation of the Rhine.
