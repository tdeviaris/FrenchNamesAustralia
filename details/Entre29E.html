Located at the northern exit of the channel, Cap de la Sortie is marked in the atlas of the voyage at the northern end of Bruny Island where the d’Entrecasteaux Channel ends and merges with the Tasman Sea (hence ‘la Sortie’, The Exit).

In February 1793, the frigates anchored near the ‘cap de la sortie’, serving as a meeting point for the longboats and canoes sent on missions. The latitudes recorded by the officers at the “Cap de la Sortie” helped to determine the lengths of the D’Entrecasteaux Channel.
