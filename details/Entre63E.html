On December 8, 1792, the expedition sailed along the southwest coast of New Holland, heading toward the Recherche Archipelago while continuing its exploration and charting of the coasts it passed. D’Entrecasteaux reports: “On the morning of the 8th we saw two reefs in front of us; we passed between these and the mainland. We soon discovered a small island, which, at close examination, seemed surrounded by reefs which extended to a very low point of the mainland. On that point, which was composed mainly of sand, we saw smoke in three or four places. We were forced to pass seaward of the small island and of a rock that was close by …”
