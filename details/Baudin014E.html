Latreille worked at the Muséum d’Histoire Naturelle and assisted Lamarck in his public lectures on invertebrates. Further information: Baudin called the cape Pointe Sans Espoir (Cape without hope), because ‘of the calm and a faint north and north-westerly breeze we were thus prevented from making any great advance and were even carried away from the coast’. A cape in Western Australia also bears the name of Latreille.
