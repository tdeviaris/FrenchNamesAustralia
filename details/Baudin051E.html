On the ICSM map, Cap Montalivet is located on Kangaroo Island (east of Nepean Bay). In Freycinet atlas, it is positioned in the southeast of Fleurieu Peninsula, the present-day Newland Head. Napoléon believed that Montalivet was ‘a trustworthy man who has remained, I believe, always tenderly attached to me’ p.68. (‘ un honnête homme qui m’est demeuré, je crois, toujours tendrement attaché ‘) Freycinet added Montalivet’s name to the list of names selected for the nomenclature, in an additional list entitled ‘nomenclature’s complement’ (see annexe).

Further information: A group of islands in Western Australia bears the same historical name: East Montalivet Island: 14˚16’S; 125˚18’E, West Montalivet island: 14˚18S; 125˚13’E
