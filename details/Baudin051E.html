Cap Montalivet – Newland Head

Îles Montalivet - East Montalivet Island and West Montalivet Island

Montalivet helped to strengthen Paris’s standing as a European cultural capital. He contributed to major architectural and urban projects, including the Arc de Triomphe, the Madeleine, and the Palais Brongniart, now the seat of the French stock exchange, and supported the development of public fountains and the sewer system. When he was appointed head of the Corps des Ponts et Chaussées, the administration responsible for roads, bridges, and major public works, he met Napoléon frequently to organise infrastructure projects and submitted regular reports on the progress of public works. These included the extension of the canal network intended to link the major rivers of France, notably the Seine and the Rhône, with those of Europe, especially the Meuse and the Rhine, in order to stimulate agricultural and industrial trade. He also supported the development of a national industry capable of promoting substitute products within Europe in response to shortages caused by the British blockade.
