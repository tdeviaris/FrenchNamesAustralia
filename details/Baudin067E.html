Madeleine de Scudéry’s writings that gave rise to scientific questions continued to be discussed with passion. For example, her dialogues ‘On Butterflies and History of Two Chameleons’ defends, unlike Descartes*’s mechanistic theory of the animal-machine, a vitalist theory of animal life. She noted and observed the emotions that her two native African chameleons were displaying, in the environment of her own home, which indicated to her that they possessed a great sense of morality and affection for each other.
