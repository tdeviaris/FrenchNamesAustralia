Using his sister as an instrument of diplomatic conquest, but with her obliging assistance, Napoléon remarried Pauline, in 1803, to the Roman prince Camille Borguèse, to reinforce his relationships with the most influential Italian families. Borguèse commissioned the celebrated sculptor Antonio Canova to produce a statue of his wife who was represented as Venus, the ancient goddess of beauty (see Pauline as Venus with the apple that she offered to Paris in her hand, by Antonio Canova, 1804-1809 https://histoire-image.org/etudes/pauline-borghese-princesse-muse ).
