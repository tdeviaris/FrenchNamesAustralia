Laetitia's husband was part of the island of Corsica’s aristocracy but aligned himself with the nobility of the Ancient Regime in France. Laetitia became a widow when four of her eight children were still very young and dependant. Napoléon was her second child. During their studies and early careers, Napoléon and his eldest brother, Joseph, supported their mother and their family financially. During the French Revolution, the family had to flee from Corsica and settle in Marseille.
