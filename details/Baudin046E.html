The French did not name the large bay where this meeting occurred in the publications and atlases of the voyage, the charts produced aboard his ship, or in Baudin’s journal, possibly because the encounter had been a source of great concern, disenchantment, and doubt for the mission.

The encounter between Baudin and Flinders has been documented in both captains' journals and can now be heard on the South Australian Maritime Museum's 'First Voyages' website

Péron gave Mollien additional place names, probably because he knew that Mollien played a central role in funding the publication of the voyage: see Baie Mollien in SA and Ile Adèle (his wife) and Cap Mollien in WA.

Further information: The Ramindjeri clan of the Ngarrindjeri people had long before the European named the bay and its surroundings.
