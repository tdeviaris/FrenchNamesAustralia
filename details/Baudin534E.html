Récif Brué - Brue Reef

Brué later became acknowledged for his cartographical work. To engrave his maps, he drew directly on the copper plate, a process that ensured greater accuracy and gave the outlines more precision and sharpness; he referred to these engravings as “encyprotypes”. He is best known for his Atlas universel de géographie physique, politique, ancienne et moderne. He also produced a map of Australia, which he dedicated to his friend Freycinet.
