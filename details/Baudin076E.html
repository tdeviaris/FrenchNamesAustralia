Cap Adèle - Oyster Point

Hortense commissioned a stele at the scene of the accident that claimed the life of her close friend with the message: ‘Here, Madame la Baronne de Broc, aged 25, perished in front of her friend, on June 10, 1813. O you who visit these places, proceed with caution over these canyons: think of those who love you!’

Pointe Adèle might also refer to the half-sister of Hortense and Eugène, Marie Adélaide, also known as Adèle, born in 1786. She was the illegitimate daughter whom Alexandre de Beauharnais had with Mme des Chapelles. Adèle became at the turn of the nineteenth century Joséphine’s protégée and one of the regular guests at La Malmaison. She was married in December 1804, two days after Napoleon’s coronation, to Captain Le Comte, who later received the office of tax collector in the city of Sarlat.

Further information: The name Adèle does not appear in the first rough charts of the gulf, drawn by Boullanger and Freycinet. Péron probably met Adèle Auguié or Adèle de Beauharnais during one of his visits to La Malmaison.
