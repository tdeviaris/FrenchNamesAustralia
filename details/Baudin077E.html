Anne Dacier , together with her husband, also translated the thoughts of the roman philosopher-emperor Marcus Aurelius Antoninus (Meditations), that were reedited in 1800. The wisdom of this emperor may have helped to maintain a relative peace and stability in the Roman Empire during his reign.

Further information: During the Napoleonic time, another significant translator of ancient Greek was the historian and philologist, Bon-Joseph Dacier (1742 –1833), who served as curator of the National Library and was a member of the Institut*. Napoléon commissioned him to prepare a report on the history of the development of ancient literature and history. Its introduction was published in 1810 under the title, Rapport historique sur les progrès de l'histoire et de la littérature ancienne. It became an authoritative text. However, as only the names of women have been given to localities in the Gulf Joséphine, it is probable that the bay has been named after Anne Dacier.
