Pierre-André Latreille worked at the Muséum d’Histoire naturelle and assisted Jean-Baptiste de Lamarck in his public lectures on invertebrates.

His colleagues referred to him as Entomologiae Princeps, “the prince of entomology”. François Péron relied on Latreille’s work to identify the insects and crustaceans collected during the voyage, particularly his articles published in the Annales du Muséum, the Encyclopédie méthodique and the Dictionnaire classique d’histoire naturelle, as well as his taxonomic works: Genera Crustaceorum et Insectorum, 4 vols., 1806–1809, and Précis des caractères génériques des insectes, 1796.
