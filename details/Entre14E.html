Port Esperance was guarded at its entrance by an island, named by d’Entrecasteaux Île La Haye, after Félix Lahaye, also spelled Lahay, Lahaie or La Haye, or Delahaye, (1767–1829). Lahaye was appointed as the expedition’s gardener on the recommendation of the professor of botany and administrator of the Musée national d’histoire naturelle*, André Thouin*, on account of his skills and probity. Gardeners on early scientific voyages often had a considerable knowledge and expertise of botany and were sometimes rightly referred to as gardener-botanists. Lahaye assisted Labillardière* in his work and accompanied him on most of his field trips but he also made his own collections of plant specimens and recorded valuable botanical information in his journal and notebooks. He brought back to France 36 boxes, containing hundreds of species of plants, including 50 Australian plants, many of them belonging to the genus Acacia.
