Cap Lévêque - Cape Leveque

Lévêque was a member of the Institut National des Sciences et des Arts. After spending two years at sea, he became a specialist in the determination of longitude, drawing in particular on the work of Jean-Charles de Borda and the astronomer Joseph Jérôme Lalande (see Cap Lalande, in South Australia). Building on their teaching, he published in 1779 a practical navigation manual, Le guide du navigateur, ou Traité de la pratique des observations et des calculs nécessaires au navigateur, in which he incorporated Borda’s method for calculating longitude at sea from celestial angles, along with Lalande’s astronomical tables.

After the French Revolution, he was compelled for a time to remain in hiding; nevertheless, because of his scientific reputation, he was later recruited as an examiner for candidates seeking admission to the École polytechnique and to the Navy. Some of the officers of the Baudin expedition who had passed through that school may have been examined by him.
