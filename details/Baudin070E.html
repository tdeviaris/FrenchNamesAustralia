As they continue their mission to explore the two gulfs, Freycinet and his crew reached the head of the gulf, (Gulf St Vincent). Hoping to discover the mouth of a major river, they found only low-lying and flooded shores, without major estuaries. In his notebook, the geographer of the Baudin expedition, Boullanger*, reported on January 15–16, 1803: ‘The head of this first gulf ends in such lowlands that, even in only 2 or 3 fathoms of water, we could see only drowned trees on the banks [in fact, Avicennia marina]’

Further information: Today, this mangrove in the Upper Gulf St Vincent Marine Park with the Light River Delta is considered one of the most ecologically intact saltmarsh systems in South Australia.
