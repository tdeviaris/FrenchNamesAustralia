Tourville and Duquesne are represented in armor with their breastplate, made of plates articulated assembled with hinges, and with a scarf wrapped around the neck, tied in front.

The names Tourville and Dufresne are associated in the nomenclature not only because both men embody the greatness of the French Navy, but also because of the institutional context of their time. After the Revolutionary period, which had profoundly undermined the State’s control over the training of naval officers, Napoleon and his Minister of the Navy, Decrès, undertook to re-establish schools specifically dedicated to the education of naval officers — men who combined naval expertise with military discipline. By the decree of 27 September 1810, two special naval schools were thus created and installed directly on-board ships: one at Brest, on the Tourville, and the other at Toulon, on the Duquesne.
