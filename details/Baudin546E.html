Île Commerson – Commerson Island

Philibert Commerson

Commerson's work was influenced by Tournefort and Desfontaines, as well as by Buffon. He is best known as the naturalist and medical practitioner on the voyage around the world commanded by Louis Antoine de Bougainville (1766-1769) aboard La Boudeuse and L'Étoile. He later became well known on Isle de France, where, under the patronage of Poivre, he worked after 1768 on the development of spice plantations, food crops, and forestry.

Commerson died before he had time to publish the results of his work. His field notebooks from Bougainville's voyage, including his documentation on cetaceans, were first transferred to Buffon in order to contribute to the Histoire naturelle. After Buffon's death, Bernard Germain de Lacépède continued this project devoted to cetaceans, oviparous quadrupeds, serpents, and fishes. He drew on Commerson's notes on cetaceans, as assembled by Buffon. He thus named Delphinus commersonii for a dolphin observed in the Strait of Magellan; then, using Péron's notes from the Baudin voyage, he named Delphinus peronii for another dolphin species observed south of Tasmania.
