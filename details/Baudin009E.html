Baudin had named this locality Cap des Représentations, explaining in his journal that: ‘I named this cape, which seemed to me the most southerly since Wilsons Promontory, Cap Répresentations, following disputes brought to me [about divergent representations and interpretations of the coasts made to Baudin] by the General Staff at that time’ [‘Je nommai le cap qui me parut être le plus au Sud depuis le Promontoire de Wilson, le cap des Représentations, sur (des) différends qui me furent (faits) par l’Etat Major dans ce même moment.’ ]

An island in Western Australia and a bay in South Australia were also named after Suffren, reflecting of Napoléon’s high regard for Suffren’s naval ability.
