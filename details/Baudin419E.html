Hamelin selected Moreau for the voyage for his mathematical skills. He wrote in his journal: ‘I have assigned the midshipman Moreau, who shows a lot of willingness and disposition, to Ensign St. Cricq to do the watch. This young officer, who is very good at manoeuvring and has a good command, will give Midshipman Moreau the tone I want him to take as commander, before entrusting him with the conduct of a watch’ [‘J'ai adjoint le cen Moreau aspirant qui manifeste beaucoup de bonne volonté et de disposition, à l'enseigne St Cricq pr faire le quart. Ce jeune offer qui manœuvre très bien et qui a un bon commandement donnera à l'aspirant Moreau le ton que je lui désire voir prendre en commandant, auparavant de lui confier la conduite d'un quart’]. Originally from Saint-Domingue, Moreau was promoted to ensign in Timor in 1801, and, after the voyage, to lieutenant, in 1805.

When le Naturaliste and le Geographe were reunited (21 September 1801) in Kumpang Bay (Timor) after being separated following a major storm in Geographe bay, Moreau developed a strong friendship with the officer Charles Baudin, on board le Geographe, and the midshipman Duval d’Ailly. Charles Baudin writes in his Memoirs:

“This was the time of the beginnings of my friendship with Duval d’Ailly and Moreau, both midshipmen on board the Naturaliste. Until then, we had scarcely seen each other, even during our stopovers at Tenerife and Mauritius.”

This friendship grew in Port Jackson, “during which time the two ships remained at anchorage together in a part of the port a long way from the city… My admiration for the great qualities of Moreau amounted to real hero-worship.”

Moreau died prematurely in action. The Minister of the Navy, Decrès, reports: “Sire, Lieutenant Charles Moreau was killed on 8 March 1808 in a battle fought by Your Majesty's frigate La Piémontaise against the San-Fiorenzo. At the time of his death, this officer had served for approximately ten years... His talents, his courage, and his noble ambition gave rise to the highest hopes...”
