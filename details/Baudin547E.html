Claude de Forbin (1656-1733), known as the Chevalier de Forbin, fought in numerous naval engagements. He served, for example, under d’Estrées in the West Indies in 1679-1680, with Jean Bart in the English Channel, and with Duguay-Trouin in the North Sea in 1691, although their relationship later turned bitter. He also took part in the Battle of Lagos in 1693 during the Nine Years’ War. The fact that the names of d’Estrées, Jean Bart, Duguay-Trouin, and Forbin were all included in the nomenclature highlights the importance attached to the great figures of French naval history.

Forbin also served, in 1685, in the fleet charged with conveying an ambassador and several Jesuits to Narai, king of Siam. Remaining there for three years, he was appointed admiral and general by the king of Siam before returning to Pondicherry and then to France. Disapproved of by Narai’s minister, he wrote: “I was so glad to leave that accursed country that, in the moment, I forgot all that I had suffered there.”
