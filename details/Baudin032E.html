Lannes was celebrated not only for his exceptional courage, but also for his enduring humanity. Though he faithfully executed the Emperor’s orders, he did not hesitate to voice his aversion to unnecessary cruelty. (especially the burning of the village of Binasco) and the ruthless suppression that accompanied the Napoleonic wars. In the aftermath of Austerlitz, he wrote to his wife: ‘Nous avons tout culbuté, c’est-à-dire tout tué ou pris: on n’a jamais vu un carnage pareil‘ (‘We have all collapsed, captured or dead: we have never seen carnage like this’).

Further information: Baudin called the cape Pointe Marbrée (marbled), probably because of the alternation of lighter and darker coloured sedimentary sequences that form the cape (see the profile below).
