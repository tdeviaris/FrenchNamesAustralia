Banc des Holothuries – Holothuria bank

The documentation of this specialised form of fishing and trade formed part of the expedition’s assigned work. In his report, Péron devoted five detailed pages to the Malay sailing vessels known as proas, to the methods used for harvesting sea cucumbers, and to the techniques of desiccation and drying carried out beneath the tropical sun.

Such information was of real interest to European commercial ambitions in the region, particularly because of the trading networks linking these shores to China. There, sea cucumbers were prized as aphrodisiacs and sought after, in Péron’s words, “with such eagerness and ardour.” He also observed that several species of holothurians were found in European waters and elsewhere in the world, but that they were not regarded there as commercial goods.

Péron classified holothurians as “particular zoophytes.” Cuvier later placed them within the first class of zoophytes, the echinoderms, and more specifically among the pedicellate forms — those furnished with feet. Holothurians possess rows of leg-like podia. In modern invertebrate classification, Holothuria is the type genus of the marine animal family Holothuriidae.
