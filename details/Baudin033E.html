Rivoli Bay, surveyed on 6 April 1802, entitled ‘Carte de la Côte Sud Ouest de la Nouvelle Hollande, dressée à bord de la Corvette le Géographe, Cinquième feuille’ (Map of the South West Coast of New Holland, drawn up on board the Corvette le Géographe , Fifth sheet), manuscript cartographic document, ANF/ MAP/6JJ/4/B piece 79 Source : Archives nationales de France : https://www.siv.archives-nationales.culture.gouv.fr/siv/UD/FRAN_IR_060973/map6JJ_429

While Péron was working on the new nomenclature of the voyage, Napoleon had a commemorative monument erected in 1806 on the battlefield at Rivoli — a twenty-metre-high column. It was, however, demolished by the Austrians in 1814. Further information: Baudin called this bay ‘Baie de Quiberon’ (the name of a peninsula in Britany). He also named a reef in the bay ‘La Chaussee’ (The Pavement) which, according to Péron, was detected at the very last moment before the ship was going to run aground, which would have resulted in shipwreck with dire consequences for the crew. The outer part of this reef and other rocky patches are now forming part of Ringwood reef. A group of islands in Western Australia also bear the name of the battle, ‘Ȋles de Rivoli’.
