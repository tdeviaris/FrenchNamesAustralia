These two bays are located at the south-Western end of Bruny Island. La Grande Anse and La Petite Anse, meaning the Great Bay and the Little Bay, are descriptive topographical names given to two adjacent coves in the south-west of Bruny Island. Their known location helps navigators to chart their route through the D’Entrecasteaux Channel. The officer de Cretin, who carried out the first coastal reconnaissance of these coves, reported that in May 1792 his party reached Cape Bruny by rowing very hard and, after passing it, they discovered first a sandy cove with two steep rocky points marking its entrance (Great Taylors Bay). He noted that they were only able to step ashore in the next and smaller cove (Little Taylors Bay).
