Lieutenant Saint-Aignan and the hydrographer Beautemps-Beaupré noticed that medium sized vessels could seek shelters from any kind of wind in the Baie des Moules, if they anchored behind two islets or rocks, now known as Pelican Islands in Deephole Bay, in water 4 or 5 fathoms deep and with a sandy bottom. They also ‘found a great quantity of mussels and other shellfishs on the bank of a kind of river or canal with salty water.’ They were probably referring to the narrow opening on the western side of Baie des Moules leading to Hasting Bay, unless they saw the shells along the narrow entrance to a side arm of Baie des Moules opening close to the little islands (Pelican Islands), taking on the form of a ‘river or canal’.
