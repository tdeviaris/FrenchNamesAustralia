Freycinet describes the island from a more hydrographic perspective than Péron, who was primarily a naturalist”: “At the western end of Tourville Bay lies Dragon Island, so named for its strange shape. The channel that separates it from the mainland appeared to us safe and easy to navigate, although narrow. This island is, moreover, nothing more than a barren rock, near which there are two others that seem to be connected to the first by underwater rocks.”
