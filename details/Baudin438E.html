Cap Ransonnet - Cape Ransonnet

Baudin promoted Ransonnet to acting sub-lieutenant on Hamelin’s recommendation in October 1801, but on 21-22 May 1802 the commander demoted him to the rank of second-class midshipman. Ransonnet was later transferred to La Casuarina at Timor in May 1803, before being appointed sub-lieutenant at Île de France on 26 October 1803.

Relations between Ransonnet and Baudin were difficult: they seem to have been marked by resentment on the part of the former and by cynicism on that of the latter. In Sydney, for example, Baudin placed Ransonnet under short confinement for failing to comply with orders, after he had allowed one of Le Géographe’s boats to remain away from the corvette beyond the authorized hour of 9 p.m. Baudin then sent him an ironic note: “... you will kindly confine yourself in meditation in your bedroom [crossed out in the text] in the depths of your room, and try to persuade yourself that, should such a thing happen again in the future, you will have more time to meditate at length.”

In February 1803, Ransonnet also saw his cabin confiscated by Baudin to house captive kangaroos.

Despite these tensions, Ransonnet was entrusted with several significant missions during the expedition, notably in Tasmania, on Kangaroo Island — see the entry Anse des Hauts-Fonds — in South Australia, and in Western Australia — see the entry Cape Ransonnet.
