French cartoon published in 1780 [engraving] ‘ in Recueil. Collection Michel Hennin. Estampes relatives à l'Histoire de France. Tome 112, Pièces 9732-9831, période : 1779-1780 The ‘Destin’ (‘Destiny’) is the name given to d’Estaing in the cartoon – both names have the same pronunciation in French. D’Estaing is shown leading the British presented in the form of animals on leashes and farming America is depicted in the traits of a native sitting on tobacco barrels and cases of rice and indigo that are intended for France and French allies. The native holds a baton topped by a Phrygian cap, while the trumpet of Fame puts a crown on his head proclaiming ‘Vive D’Estaing’.’ Detail Bibliothèque nationale de France http://catalogue.bnf.fr/ark:/12148/cb40492993t
