Cap Ronsard - Cape Ronsard

Île Ronsard - Ronsard Island

Although embarked as a marine engineer, Ronsard fulfilled much broader naval functions during the voyage. His initial technical responsibility is reflected in the construction of replacement longboats: first at Timor, after the loss of the Géographe’s longboat in Geographe Bay, and then again after the Timor-built longboat was lost in Bass Strait on 15 December 1802, swallowed by the sea as the Géographe was getting under way from King Island. But his activity was not limited to shipbuilding. He took part in reconnaissance missions, made soundings, drew charts and kept highly informative journals. In particular, he was entrusted with a mission to explore Depuch Island in July 1801: there he described the basaltic formations with great precision, collected natural-history specimens and drew a chart. However, it was not this island that received his name, but a smaller island nearby, close to Depuch Island, which he had in fact not had time to reconnoitre.

During the stopover at Timor, in August-September 1801, he was given the functions of sub-lieutenant and then lieutenant.

Still at Timor, but this time in May 1803, Ronsard became involved in a serious dispute with Henri Freycinet over seniority and command aboard the Géographe. Ronsard maintained that, as a naval engineer with long-standing brevet seniority, he ranked above Freycinet and should remain first officer during Baudin’s absences, with a claim to command the ship should Baudin die. Freycinet contested this claim, refusing to be placed under the authority of an engineer. Baudin, whose health was deteriorating, settled the matter by consulting the crew and the naturalists in a secret vote. Freycinet was chosen by 60 votes to 12 and was designated to take command of the ship if necessary. Ronsard regarded this decision as a dishonourable demotion and asked to leave the expedition, but Baudin refused to put him ashore. He therefore continued the voyage solely in his original capacity as naval engineer, independent of the ship’s senior officers and answerable directly to Baudin.
