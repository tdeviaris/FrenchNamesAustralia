Îles Emériau - Emeriau Point

In 1812, Napoléon Bonaparte promoted Émeriau to the rank of vice-admiral. At the beginning of 1813, he was appointed Inspector-General of the Mediterranean coasts and, shortly thereafter, named a Grand Officer of the Legion of Honour.

In 1813, Toulon and the surrounding coastline were confronted with the blockade of an enemy fleet carrying around 20,000 men intended for a landing. Thanks to Émeriau’s strategic abilities, in coordination with André Masséna, the enemy was held at bay.

At Saint Helena, Napoléon confided that, having recognised Émeriau’s administrative abilities, he had even considered appointing him as minister in place of Denis Decrès (p. 297).
