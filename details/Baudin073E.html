Julie de Lespinasse and d’Alembert were close friends, and both were upset when Denis Diderot, in his philosophical work D’Alembert’s Dream*, constructed a fictitious debate on materialist theories between the two, using them as voices for his own ideas. He attributed statements to them such as, ‘To be born, to live, and to pass away is to change forms’ (attributed by Diderot to d’Alembert), and ‘Everything changes and passes away; only the whole remains unchanged. The world is ceaselessly beginning and ending; at every moment, it is at its beginning and its end. There has never been any other world, and there never will be’ (attributed to Mlle de Lespinasse).
