Île Duguesclin – Duguesclin Island

Baie Duguesclin - Tiparra Bay

Bertrand du Guesclin, also spelled Duguesclin (c. 1320-1380), was a knight who distinguished himself notably at the Battle of Cocherel in 1364, a victory that helped restore the authority of the French king, which had been challenged by regional rebellions and by English claims. Du Guesclin employed more mobile and effective methods of warfare, relying less on spectacular chivalry than on attrition, sieges, mobility, and gradual reconquest. His deeds lived on in legends and in songs composed after his death by the poet Cuvelier.

Napoleon admired Du Guesclin for his political and military achievements and regarded him as one of the great figures in French history. He became a symbol of heroism, and his legendary helmet was said to have been used to hold the medals of the Legion of Honour during award ceremonies. (Established by Napoleon in 1802, the Legion of Honour is the highest French order of merit; see the glossary.)
