Île Haüy - Hauy Island

Cap Haüy - Cape Hauy

Haüy is known as the father of modern crystallography. He began his research at the time when Lavoisier was studying the elements in chemistry; together they contributed to the development of a science of matter and, in the nineteenth century, to the elaboration of atomic theory. Haüy adopted a systematic method to analyse the structure of crystals, a method that Péron takes up in his descriptions of rocks, stressing the geometric regularity of their forms, their rhomboidal character, and the decomposition of masses into analogous elementary units.

Haüy succeeded Dolomieu in his teaching position at the Muséum national d’histoire naturelle after the latter’s death.

During his captivity at Longwood, on Saint Helena, Napoleon enjoyed rereading Haüy’s lectures on crystallography and cosmogony.
