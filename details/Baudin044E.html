Tome 94, Pièces 8171-8249, période : 1732-1736 Source : Bibliothèque nationale de France, http://catalogue.bnf.fr/ark:/12148/cb415070303 He is wearing a refined suit together with his military attributes: his sword and his Marshal’s baton.

Confident in his lucky star, he proclaimed himself ‘the only general in Europe whose happiness was never altered in war’. Baudin named the cape ‘Cap Le Lièvre’ (hare).

An island in South Australia was also dedicated to Villars.
