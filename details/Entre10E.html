Baie mauvaise , meaning ‘bad bay’, on the southern coast of Bruny Island, owes its name to the unfavorable impression it left on Lieutenant de Cretin. Sent on 30 April 1792 to carry out a second reconnaissance mission, following his survey of Recherche Bay*, he found the shores of this broad inlet so steep and inaccessible that no landing could be made to take bearings. Judging that the bay could offer no shelter for ships, he abandoned the survey and continued northward to examine a large inlet previously sighted to the northeast of Recherche Bay (pp. 67–68).
