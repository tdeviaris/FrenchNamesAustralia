Canal Degérando – West Basin

Joseph Marie Degérando (1772-1842), also known as de Gérando, gained a high reputation after the publication of his memoir on signs and on the way their symbolic forms and other representations shape human thought, Des signes et de l'art de penser considérés dans leurs rapports mutuels (see also Île Lancelin, Western Australia). After this essay won the prize in the competition organised by the Institut National des Sciences et des Arts in 1799, the Institut and the Société des Observateurs de l'Homme commissioned him to write a memoir intended to guide the study of the ways of life, beliefs, and values of Indigenous peoples, entitled Considérations sur les diverses méthodes à suivre dans l'observation des peuples sauvages.

Péron attempted to draw on the recommendations set out in this work in his study of the Aboriginal peoples of Bruny Island and Maria Island. At the time when Péron and Freycinet were working on the nomenclature, they may have encountered Degérando, who was then serving as secretary to Jean-Baptiste de Nompère de Champagny.
