The transmission of Antoinette Deshoulières’s poetic legacy appears to have been safeguarded by closely connected intellectual circles. The family of Victorine de Chastenay, by preserving original manuscripts, likely played a pivotal role in disseminating her verse. It is plausible that Madame de Chastenay herself introduced François Péron to these texts, thereby perpetuating the influence of a literary voice as singular as it was inspiring.
