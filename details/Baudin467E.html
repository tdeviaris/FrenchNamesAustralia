Île Faure - Faure Island

Like Boullanger, Pierre-Ange-François-Xavier Faure had studied at the École polytechnique. He embarked aboard the Naturaliste as chief engineer-geographer. His work was central to the charting of the Australian coasts, as shown by his notebooks of bearings and the manuscript charts he produced during the expedition.

Unlike Boullanger, however, Faure did not take part in the editorial preparation of the voyage atlas in Paris. On the expedition’s return journey, he disembarked at Île-de-France, where he remained for the rest of his life. He founded a family there and pursued a civilian career, notably as a pharmacist and mathematics teacher.
