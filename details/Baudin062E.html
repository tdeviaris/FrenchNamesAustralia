In his attempt to restore the original French names to geographical features that had not been first visited and named by Flinders, Alphonse de Fleurieu noted: ‘… In South Australia, they are a bit weary of long names … Jeanne Hachette will lose [her identity] if only her first name is used … On the other hand, [the full name of the] highly regarded Jeanne d’Arc [should been kept]—she even has her own statue in Melbourne …’ However, unsurprisingly, given their political symbolism, neither of these two names has been reinstated.
