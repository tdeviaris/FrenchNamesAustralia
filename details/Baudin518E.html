Cap Gourdon - Cape Gourdon

Cap Villaret - Cape Villaret

Île Gantheaume - Gantheaume Point

These places are part of the Pilbara coast in the nearby south of Broome. Île Gantheaume is not an island, but a small peninsula. From the distance, Freycinet noted that from Cape Latouche Treville to Cape Villaret, the coast is ‘pleasant’.

The four names relate to Navy commanders who took part in the Saint-Domingue expedition (1801-1803), (today’s Haiti), the military campaign launched by Napoléon Bonaparte and led by General Leclerc (see the entry Pointe Pauline*, in SA), to suppress the ‘revolution’ led by Toussaint Louverture in Santo Domingo. The latter had proclaimed an innovative autonomist constitution on July 8, 1801.

Immersed in the Napoleonic propaganda, Péron and Freycinet celebrated senior officers who had been part of the Saint-Domingue expedition despite their first campaign ending in defeat, and with Saint-Domingue winning its independence in 1804, marking the first major defeat of le Consulat and the only example of a successful slave revolt.
