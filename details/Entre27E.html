Cap Le Grand, later renamed Kinghorne Point, forms the westernmost projection of North Bruny Island into the central part of the D’Entrecasteaux Channel. Jacques Bernard Le Grand (1763-1798), born in Bergerac in the Dordogne, was a lieutenant on the Esperance. During the expedition’s stay in Tasmania, he distinguished himself by his work in surveying and charting its coasts and bays in company with lieutenant La Seinie and the hydrographer Jouvency. In his journal, Labillardière noted that he was ‘one of the best officers of the expedition’* (See the entry Cape Le Grand*, in the Archipelago of La Recherche).
