Île Verte or Green Island is a spot of land, some 400m in length and less than 200m wide, located in the D’Entrecasteaux Channel, midway between Bruny Island and mainland Tasmania. It is now a nature reserve. Labillardière referred to it in his journal as ‘a little round island’, where the gardener, Lahaye, and his party sought refuge against adverse winds. The island’s name is probably derived from the green plants of bracken and thistle which cover much of its surface.
