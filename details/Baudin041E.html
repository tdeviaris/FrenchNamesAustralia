The page on which Fermat remarked that the margin was too narrow to contain the proof of his conjecture was, in fact, page 61 of Claude-Gaspard Bachet de Méziriac’s Latin translation of Diophantus’ Arithmetica. In this margin, Fermat wrote: “To divide a cube into two other cubes, a fourth power, or in general any power whatever into two powers of the same degree above the second, is impossible; and I have assuredly discovered a truly marvellous proof of this, which this margin is too narrow to contain.” Later, his son republished Arithmetica, incorporating Fermat’s marginal annotations into the new edition.

Further information: Baudin named cape Fermat Pointe du Rossignol’ (nightingale). It was first replaced in the rough charts with the toponym Cap Regnard, after Jean-François Regnard, a 17th century writer and comedian whose name was later attributed to a bay in Western Australia. An island in South Australia was also named by Freycinet Ȋle Fermat
