Cap Richelieu - Cape Schanck

Ile Richelieu - Franklin Island

In Napoleonic time, Richelieu was seen as the personality who restore the royal authority after the Wars of Religion and the assassination of the king Henri IV, and whose policy aimed to make France a European leader. Richelieu was an important person to the Navy as he established the French Royal Navy and founded the French trading companies based on the model of the Dutch East India Company (VOC) to allow France to control and develop its trading expansion. Richelieu created the French Academy (l'Academie francaise) in 1635, that controls French language to make it a better instrument of thought.

More generally, Richelieu was acknowledged for establishing centralized administrative structures that strengthened the State's power. His principles in implementing organizational reform were in phase with those later adopted by Napoleon.

Further information: The cape named Richelieu by the French now bears the name of Cape Schanck, after Captain John Schank RN, an engineer who introduced sliding keels on ships used by the British Royal Navy. One of these ships was the 60-ton brig Lady Nelson, built at Deptford in 1799 and used in the early survey of the south-east coast of Australia by James Grant in 1801. See the ICSM map section in the next entry.

The present name Franklin Islands was given by Matthew Flinders on 3 February 1802 in honour of John Franklin, then a midshipman on HMS Investigator, who later became Sir John Franklin, the celebrated Arctic explorer and Lieutenant-Governor of Van Diemen's Land.
