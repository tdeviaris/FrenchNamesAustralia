Section of the above chart ‘Chart of the south-west coast of New Holland constructed on board le Géographe…’ showing the line of dangerous breakers that prevented the expedition from landing and frightened Péron April 1802, sheet 4. MAP/6JJ/4/B/78. Source: Archives nationales de France

Carpenter Rocks are structurally controlled trending parallel with the Robe Range. Source : Google Earth

Baudin notes: ‘A bonus of one louis was given to Ciré, the topman, who was the first to sight the rocky coast and to give the warning’ (1-2 April 1802). Today, these breakers allow sailors to precisely locate the Carpenter rocks, which Baudin’s crew had sighted and avoided.
