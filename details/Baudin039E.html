Using Lacépède’s classification of cetaceans and fishes to categorise the new species, off the Terre de Witt, Péron explained: “However perilous this navigation may have been, it could still not tear M. Lesueur* and me away from our usual labours, and this last day (15th August 1801) was marked by an important discovery: that of a new species of fish (Balistapodus wittensis N.). It is close to the balistes but differs from them in the complete absence of any kind of ventral fin. This last characteristic makes it the first type specimen of a new order within the ichthyological system of my illustrious master, M. de Lacépède...” However, the French entomologist Girard, who specialised more broadly in invertebrate animals, noted in 1856: “[Péron] indeed believed, upon encountering a triggerfish lacking ventral fins, that he had realized one of Lacépède’s theoretical conceptions—for whom he professed a kind of veneration—and he named it Balistapodus wiltensis. It is now known that triggerfishes have ventral fins that are sometimes well developed and sometimes rudimentary. The fish discovered by Péron and Lesueur belongs to the latter case, which explains how these fins went unnoticed.” [« [Péron] crut, en effet, en recontrant un baliste dépourvu de nageoires ventrales, avoir réalisé une des conceptions théoriques de Lacépède, pour qui il professait une sorte de vénération, et le nomma balistapodus wiltensis. On sait maintenant que les balistes ont des nageoires ventrales tantôt développées, tantôt rudimentaires. Le poisson découvert par Péron et Lesueur est dans ce dernier cas, ce qui explique comment ces nageoires restèrent inaperçues. »] Péron asserted also that he followed the method of Lacépède who ‘compared all the broad physiological relationships of these animals and determined all the possible combinations of their principal external organs…’. He overall honored Lacépède generously with also two other toponyms: the Détroit de Lacépède in South Australia and the Île Lacépède in Western Australia.

Further information: Baudin originally named the bay Baie des Orphelins (orphans).
