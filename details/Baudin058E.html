The 1802 marriage of Hortense to Napoléon’s brother, Louis Bonaparte, was strategically arranged to forge alliances between Joséphine’s family and the Bonaparte siblings. However, tensions soon arose, as Napoléon’s brothers and sisters—particularly Caroline, his younger sister—grew increasingly distrustful of both their stepmother and Hortense. Napoléon, for his part, sought to establish a dynastic hierarchy designed to consolidate control over Europe.

Further information: Baudin named the Bay Baie des Savoyards, an allusion to the annexation of the Duchy of Savoy by the French Republic in 1792.
