Cap Réaumur - Cape Reamur

Baudin named this cape Cap Camus, most likely in honour of Armand-Gaston Camus (1740–1804), a lawyer regarded as the founder of the French National Archives. Camus played an important role during the Revolution and was known for his incorruptible republican principles, having refused Bonaparte’s offers to become Minister of Finance and later Minister of Police. This steadfastness may explain why Péron and Freycinet ultimately judged his name unsuitable for their nomenclature.
