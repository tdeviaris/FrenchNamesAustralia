Baudin named the cape ‘Cap Camus,’ likely after Armand-Gaston Camus (1740–1804), a lawyer regarded as the founder of the Archives Nationales. Camus played an important role during the Revolution and was known for his incorruptible republican principles, having declined Bonaparte’s offers to serve as finance minister and later as police minister. This steadfastness may explain why Péron and Freycinet ultimately found his name unsuitable for their nomenclature.
