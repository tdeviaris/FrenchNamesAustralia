Baie Flacourt - Flacourt Bay

The originality of Flacourt's Histoire de la grande isle Madagascar lies in its encyclopaedic ambition. The work is not limited to the account of a French colonial attempt: it offers a broad description of Madagascar, its provinces, rivers, resources, inhabitants, customs, language, plants and animals. Through this combined attention to human societies and the natural world, Flacourt appears as one of the first French authors to produce a genuine synthesis of Madagascar.

The second edition of 1661, entitled Histoire de la grande isle Madagascar, composée par le sieur de Flacourt... avec une relation de ce qui s'est passé ès années 1655, 1656 et 1657, reinforced this documentary dimension by adding an account of more recent events. It thus combined the general description of the island with a chronicle of the political, military and commercial difficulties faced by the French presence in Madagascar.

It was probably this broad understanding of natural history, encompassing places, peoples, plants and animals, together with the experience of the difficulties of French settlement in the Indian Ocean, that made the name Flacourt a particularly suggestive reference for Péron when the expedition's nomenclature was being developed.
