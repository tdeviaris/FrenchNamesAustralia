As the island was a strategic factor in the Franco-British rivalry for control of the trade routes to India, during Le Géographe’s stopover at the Isle de France on its return voyage to France in 1803, Decaen asked Péron to provide him with any information he had gathered on British colonization in New Holland. On December 12, 1803, Péron submitted a report to Decaen in which Flinders’s role as a scientific voyager was minimized, while his supposed political mission was exaggerated (Fornasiero & West-Sooby, 2004, p. 13). In this report, Péron suggested that the British might seek to colonize the Pacific region and even advocated a military action against Port Jackson, a proposal that, however, was ignored by the French authorities.
