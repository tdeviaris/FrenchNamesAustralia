Jean-Charles de Folard, known as the Chevalier de Folard, (1669-1752), after whom the landmark was later named, was a war strategist and engineer who commented, annotated, and translated Polybius from Greek into French, and whose works included major references used to master the art of war. He advocated infantry attacks in columns rather than in lines, and his theory was widely discussed in military circles. Napoléon, read his books methodically, especially ‘Nouvelles découvertes sur la guerre’ (new innovations in relation to warfare) and ‘traité de la colonne’ (treatise on the subject of military column).
