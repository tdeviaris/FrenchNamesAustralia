Cap Folard - Point Ronald

Jean-Charles de Folard, known as the Chevalier de Folard (1669–1752), to whom the place was later dedicated, was a military strategist and engineer. He commented on, annotated, and translated Polybius from Greek into French, and his works became major points of reference for the study and mastery of the art of war. He notably defended infantry attacks in columns rather than in lines, a theory that was widely debated in military circles. Napoleon read his works methodically, especially the New discoveries on warfare and the Treatise on the column.
