Jean François Sylvestre Denis de Trobriand (1765-1799), a lieutenant in the Espérance, was born in Finistère in Brittany, the son of the comte de Trobriand, whose family name was Denni. He served in the French navy under Bougainville and took part in the American War of Independence, before joining the d’Entrecasteaux expedition. On his return to France, he was given command of la Vestale and left for Saint-Domingue, where he died in 1799.

During the second stopover at Van Diemen’s Land, when the expedition’s fresh water supplies were in dire shortage, Trobriand, who had been sent on a mission, discovered a small stream of excellent water. “After two hours of work and with the help of a makeshift conduit, we were able to fill our boats with the greatest ease, as they could draw water directly beneath the spout, unhindered and regardless of the tide.”
