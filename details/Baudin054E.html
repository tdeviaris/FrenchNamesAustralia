Jean-Pierre Bourdé de La Villehuet (1732-1789) was praised for his treatise on vessels’ handlings, which was translated into English with the title ‘The Manoeuverer, or skilful seaman, being an essay on the theory and practice of the various movements of a ship at sea as well as of naval evolutions in general’. He also published more practical works and textbooks such as  Manuel des marins, ou Dictionnaire des termes de marine. His son Guillaume-François-Joseph (1763-1851) also served in the navy. He was promoted captain in May 1798, and took part in the Egyptian expedition.
