Source: DIJON, Musée des Beaux-Arts https://www.pop.culture.gouv.fr/notice/joconde/000PE008393 Crétet wears his senior official's outfit, as does Mollien. A bust of Napoléon with a laurel crown can be seen in the background. Crétet’s desk is representative of the First Empire style.

Bonaparte employed Crétet to improve the road network on Corsica, his native island, closely monitoring the progress of the work. He wrote to him on 16 February 1804: "Citizen Crétet, State Councillor, I would like to know what has been done on the road crossing Corsica, from Ajaccio to Bastia, since Vendémiaire Year XII; how much is expected to be spent on it in Year XII, and what still needs to be done so that a carriage can travel from one of these cities to the other without unhitching...(« Citoyen Crétet, conseiller d’Etat, je désirerais connaitre ce qu’il y a de fait de la route qui traverse la Corse, d’Ajaccio a Bastia, depuis vendémiaire an XII ; ce que l’on compte y dépenser dans l’An XII, et ce qu’il faut faire encore pour qu’une voiture puisse, sans dételer, se rendre de l’une à l’autre de ces villes… »)
