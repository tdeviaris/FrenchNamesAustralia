Île Bernouilli - Bernouilli Island

Cap Bernouilli - Cape Jaffa

The Bernoulli family, originally from Basel, formed one of the great learned dynasties of early modern Europe. Johann / Jean Bernoulli (1667–1748) occupied a major place within it through his contribution to the dissemination of Leibniz’s infinitesimal calculus, whose applications bore directly on astronomy, mechanics, and the sciences of navigation. He gave this orientation an explicitly nautical expression in his Essay on a New Theory of the Manoeuvre of Ships (1714), in which he applied mathematical analysis to the sailing conditions and manoeuvring of vessels. In the nomenclature of the voyage, the name Bernoulli thus seems to refer less to an isolated individual than to a learned lineage associated with the theoretical foundations of modern navigation.
