The naturalists stayed several days in the bay. The gardener Delahaye reported Spartan living conditions: frugal meals consisting only of ‘biscuit’ and cold nights. Unable to fish for abalone, they nevertheless managed to feed themselves by cooking birds hunted on site, and they built a rudimentary hut to shelter themselves from the wind while they slept.
