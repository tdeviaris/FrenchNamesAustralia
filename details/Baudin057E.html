Portrait of Mme de Rabutin Chantal, marquise de Sévigné, by Petit, engraving Mme de Sévigné enjoyed fashion and commented about dresses and hair styles in her letters to her daughter. She introduced a new hairstyle, with flat hairs on the top of the head, a thin fringe with curls on the forehead with a longer curl in the centre, and, on each side, thick curly hair falling under over the ears. Further information: Baudin did not specify the cape but named a head next to the cape Pointe aux Corbeaux (crows). It was first named by Péron and Freycinet ‘Cap d’Aboukir’. The name ‘Rapid Head’ comes from the brig Rapid, which brought Col. William Light to South Australia in 1836.
