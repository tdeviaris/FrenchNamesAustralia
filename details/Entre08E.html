One of the pages from Rossel'sbook on astronomical observation entitled ‘Observations of the elevation of the sun made on 4 January 1793’. Rossel made these observations when the expedition visited Van Diemen Land for the second time to to complete the hydrographical work started in 1792. Rossel calculates the difference in time between Paris and the ship's position and then translates this difference into degrees of longitude (bottom of document). The northeast point of the Baie des Moules was named after Élisabeth-Paul-Édouard de Rossel (1765–1829), the officer who was responsible for directing the expedition’s astronomical observations. Rossel was chosen by d’Entrecasteaux as he had previously served with distinction under his command on la Résolution. In December 1794, Rossel became the expedition’s last commander and the editor of its narrative (see the introduction).
