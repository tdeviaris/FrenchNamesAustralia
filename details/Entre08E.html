The northeast point of the Baie des Moules was named after Élisabeth-Paul-Édouard de Rossel (1765–1829), the officer who was responsible for directing the expedition’s astronomical observations. Rossel was chosen by d’Entrecasteaux as he had previously served with distinction under his command on la Résolution . In December 1794, Rossel became the expedition’s last commander and the editor of its narrative (see the introduction).
