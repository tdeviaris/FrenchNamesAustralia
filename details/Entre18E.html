Port des Cygnes, today’s Port Cygnet (the s has become a t), was the name given by d’Entrecasteaux to a sheltered bay opening on the northern banks of the Huon River, close to its mouth. It is known today as Kangaroo Bay and the name Port Cygnet lives on in that of the small town located at the head of the bay. The name Cygne (swan in English) was chosen to reflect the large quantity of black swans found there by a surveying party from one of d’Entrecasteaux’s ships. The swans, unused to the sound of shotguns, did not take off and provided the hunters with a rich supply of food.
